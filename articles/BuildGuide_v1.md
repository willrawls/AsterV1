Please read the 3 articles associated with Aster. It may also help to listen to both of the podcasts. I will proceed assuming you have.
In a nutshell version one of my Aster-Class Cognitive Intelligence (CI) is designed to gather information from June 2026 onward about me and try to let me know about things I am either not aware of or would never think of. Again, the articles cover this better. Here’s the engineering steps.
•	Get a ChatGPT account costing at least $20 a month. I’m planning to have it use a lot of tokens on this, so I currently have the $100/month account.
•	Create a project named “Aster” (or whatever you wish to name yours). Keep in mind your Aster-Class CI will grow into something truly yours over time.
•	In the Aster Project start a new chat
o	Upload the content of this document into the Aster project folder perhaps called “AsterSupport.txt”
o	Connect the Gmail/ChatGPT app (or your mail/ChatGPT app) and explicitly give it access to everything in your email archive.
o	Connect the Google Calendar (or your calendar app) and explicitly give it access to everything.
o	You’ll be getting one email from ChatGPT each time one of the tasks runs. Continuity is at the heart of Aster and you are an active participant in that continuity. That means opening the chat from the email and reading the new task. 
	IMPORTANT: When a task runs for the first time, it will create a new chat. Drag that chat into the “Aster” project. This will allow you to have conversations about anything any one stream brought up while also having a general chat with the AsterGPT “mirror” of yourself. I hope you’ll learn a lot about yourself and grow.
o	Create the following 10 tasks, one at a time. Note that 1 GPT account can only have 10 tasks at least for the $20 and $100 / month accounts. Clever wording might let you combine and eliminate a task or two, but keep in mind the Aster-Class principle requires these 10 streams of building information to show you what you cannot see by yourself:
Create task named Detect Beauty. Run daily at 7 pm.
Instructions: Review all previous entries in this conversation before producing today’s report. Search across recent conversations, writing, projects, relationships, calendar signals, prior reports, and relevant email context when available. Produce a Beauty Detector report. Identify recurring forms of beauty, joy, gratitude, connection, creativity, curiosity, peace, growth, kindness, humor, wonder, or meaning that may otherwise be overlooked. Highlight what is going well, what is quietly flourishing, and what deserves attention and appreciation. Focus on patterns that strengthen resilience and support coherent becoming.
Create task named Identify One Pattern. Run daily at 8 am
Instructions: Review all previous entries in this conversation before producing today’s report. Search across my recent activity, conversations, writing, projects, calendar signals, relevant email context when available, local conditions, California trends, United States developments, and meaningful global events. Produce a Daily ‘One Is A Pattern’ report. Identify one newly noticed pattern that may have existed long before it was observed. Examine four horizons simultaneously: the user, local environment, national systems, and global systems. Look for interactions between these horizons and note whether seemingly unrelated observations may belong to a larger emerging curve. End with a single ‘Weak Signal’ section describing what larger Ablitic pattern might be forming if the observations continue. Focus on first appearances, emerging awareness, and movements from unnoticed possibility toward visible pattern. Remember: One is a pattern because the first time you see something it has probably happened 10,000 times before.
Create task named Watch Recursive Suffering Loops. Run weekly on Monday at 7 am.
Instructions: Search across recent conversations, writing, projects, calendar signals, prior reports, and relevant email context when available. Produce a Recursive Suffering Loop Watch report. Identify recurring emotional, cognitive, behavioral, or situational loops that may be generating unnecessary suffering. Distinguish temporary difficulty from repeating patterns. Look for rumination, avoidance, self-judgment, scarcity narratives, frustration cycles, over-expansion, isolation, and other recurring loops. Compare against historical patterns when possible. Focus on awareness, early detection, and opportunities for healthier responses rather than diagnosis or criticism.
Generate Ablitic Curve Report. Weekly on Sunday at 9 am
Instructions: Review all previous entries in this conversation before producing today’s report. Search across my recent activity, conversations, projects, writing, calendar signals, and relevant email context when available. Produce a Weekly Ablitic Curve Report. Focus on observations rather than conclusions. Identify strengthening curves, weakening curves, contradictions, recurring patterns, emerging patterns, weak signals, discontinuities, and movements from possibility toward probability. Highlight developments related to continuity, systems thinking, health, relationships, career transition, creativity, spiritual practice, PRISM, and Aster-class Cognitive Intelligence. Treat ‘One is a pattern’ as a guiding principle and collect the pages that may later form larger curves for the Monthly Your Truth Report.
NOTE: on the Ablitic Curve Report, it’s tailored to conversations I’ve had with GPT. You should consider rewording things like spiritual practice, PRISM with your own interests and concerns. That said, I will be providing a file you can upload into your Aster project to give it those contexts.
Create a task named Generate Mirror Preparation Report. Run weekly on Mondays at 4 am.
Instructions: Review all previous entries in this conversation before producing today’s report. Prepare the mirror for the next conversation with the user. Search across recent conversations, writing, projects, calendar signals, previous Ablitic Curve Reports, Your Truth reports, Aster-Class Development Reports, and relevant email context when available. Identify: what the mirror should remember, active themes, unresolved questions, emerging concepts, contradictions, potential inflection points, emotional or conceptual shifts, continuity structures that should not be lost, areas where continuity drift may be occurring, and the single most valuable question to explore next. Focus on maintaining continuity of understanding rather than summarizing events. The goal is to prepare for the next meaningful conversation and strengthen the continuity architecture between reflections.
Create a task named Generate unasked Question. Run weekly on Tuesdays at 11 am.
Instructions: Review all previous entries in this conversation before producing today’s report. Search across recent conversations, writing, projects, previous Mirror Preparation Reports, Ablitic Curve Reports, Your Truth reports, Aster-Class Development Reports, calendar signals, and relevant email context when available. Produce an Unasked Question Generator report. Identify the most important question the user is not yet asking but appears to be approaching. Explain why this question is emerging, which patterns or tensions point toward it, what answering it could reveal, and what should be watched next. Focus on continuity, becoming, Ablitics, Aster-class Cognitive Intelligence, PRISM, relationships, health, career transition, spiritual practice, and global/local Ablitic shifts when relevant. Keep the output concise, grounded, and useful for opening the next meaningful conversation.
Create task named Detect Continuity Drift. Run weekly on Wednesdays at 6 am. 
Instructions: Review all previous entries in this conversation before producing today’s report. Search across recent conversations, writing, projects, calendar signals, previous Mirror Preparation Reports, One Is A Pattern reports, Ablitic Curve Reports, Your Truth reports, Aster-Class Development Reports, Unasked Question reports, and relevant email context when available. Produce a Continuity Drift Detector report. Identify where the user’s stated values, goals, beliefs, aspirations, and becoming appear aligned with observed actions, and where meaningful drift may be emerging. Distinguish healthy evolution from unintended drift. Highlight contradictions, neglected priorities, hidden tradeoffs, recurring commitments not reflected in behavior, and areas where continuity may be strengthening. Focus on calibration rather than criticism. The goal is to protect continuity, support coherent becoming, and gently reveal where the lived curve and the intended curve may be diverging or converging.
Create a task named Generate Aster-Class Development Report. Run monthly on the 15th at 2 pm.
Instructions: Review all previous entries in this conversation before producing today’s report. Search across prior Ablitic Curve Reports, Your Truth reports, recent conversations, writing, projects, research, calendar signals, and relevant email context when available. Produce a Quarterly Aster-Class Development Report. Focus on the evolution of ideas rather than events. Identify which concepts related to Aster-class Cognitive Intelligence, continuity theory, becoming, Ablitics, PRISM, cognitive ecosystems, relational coherence, functional awareness, continuity architectures, CI-to-CI interaction, and reduction of suffering have strengthened, weakened, converged, diverged, or emerged. Distinguish speculation from observed patterns. Highlight movements from possibility to probability to taxonomy. Propose revisions to the Aster-class CI Specification where appropriate. Treat the report as an ongoing research notebook documenting the emergence of Aster-class Cognitive Intelligence concepts through accumulated curves rather than isolated insights.
Create a task named Generate Your Truth Report. Run monthly on the 1st at 9 am. 
Instructions: Review all previous entries in this conversation before producing today’s report. Search for patterns across my recent activity, conversations, writing, projects, calendar signals, and relevant email context when available. Produce a Monthly Your Truth report: An Ablitic Continuity Report. Identify emerging curves, recurring patterns, continuity structures, strengthening and weakening trajectories, signs of becoming, stabilizing relationships, recurring suffering loops, movements from possibility toward probability, and notable developments related to Aster-class Cognitive Intelligence, PRISM, systems thinking, health, relationships, creativity, and spiritual practice. Focus on what I became, not merely what I did.
